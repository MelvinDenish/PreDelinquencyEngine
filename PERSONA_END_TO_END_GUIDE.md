# Pre-Delinquency Engine: End-to-End Persona Walkthrough

This guide provides a comprehensive, end-to-end framework for testing and demonstrating the Pre-Delinquency Engine during your hackathon. To show the full breadth of the system, we will walk through the platform using **four distinct personas**.

---

## 🏗️ Persona 1: The System Architect / Data Engineer (Admin)
**Goal:** Prove the infrastructure, data ingestion, and pipeline reliability.

### Flow:
1. **Infrastructure Initialization:**
   - The Admin starts the core microservices using `main.py up`.
   - **Showcase:** Open Docker Desktop or use `docker ps` to show the robust, decoupled architecture (Kafka, Zookeeper, PostgreSQL, Redis, MLflow).
   - *Hackathon Talking Point:* "We built this on a production-ready Lambda architecture. Kafka handles real-time streams while Postgres manages persistent state."

2. **Data Generation & Injection:**
   - The Admin runs `main.py generate-customers 100` and `main.py generate-transactions 5000`.
   - **Showcase:** Show the realistic data structures in the console or query the Postgres database. Highlight that the generator uses real Indian demographic data, RBI-aligned income brackets, and simulated life events (e.g., unexpected medical bills).

3. **Monitoring the Pipeline:**
   - The Admin triggers the batch preprocessing via `main.py process-batch`.
   - **Showcase:** Show the logs of Apache Flink processing the streaming transactions in real-time, calculating rolling 7-day and 30-day aggregates.

---

## 👩‍💼 Persona 2: Sarah (The Retail Banking Customer)
**Goal:** Experience the empathetic, multi-channel intervention generated by GenAI.

### Profile:
Sarah is a 32-year-old marketing executive. She has always paid her credit card on time, but recently had a medical emergency (a simulated "life event" in our data generator). Her Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio just spiked, and she missed a utility bill payment.

### Flow:
1. **The Trigger Event:**
   - Run the scoring engine on Sarah's data: `main.py score`.
   - The scoring service (using the LSTM + XGBoost ensemble) detects an anomaly in her transaction patterns and flags her.

2. **Risk Assessment:**
   - Her risk score comes back at **0.82 (High Risk)**.
   - Importantly, the **SHAP explainer** identifies *why*: "High recent medical expenditure + localized drop in cash flow."

3. **The Empathetic Intervention (GenAI in Action):**
   - The Rules Engine determines Sarah needs an immediate but gentle intervention.
   - **Showcase the Output:** Show the `n8n` workflow or the terminal logs where the `NotificationDispatcher` operates. 
   - **Email/SMS Received by Sarah (Generated by Groq LLM):** 
     > *"Hi Sarah, we noticed some unusually high medical expenses on your account recently. We know life happens, and Barclays is here to support you. Would you like to explore our flexible payment plan options for this month to ease the burden? Reply 'HELP' or tap this link."*
   - *Hackathon Talking Point:* "We aren't just sending a standard 'Payment Due' text. The system uses GenAI to tailor the message based on the SHAP explanation, approaching her with empathy rather than hostility."

---

## 👨‍💻 Persona 3: David (The Relationship Manager / Collections)
**Goal:** Use AI-driven insights to manage accounts efficiently before they default.

### Profile:
David manages a portfolio of retail accounts. He needs to prioritize who to call and needs context before he picks up the phone.

### Flow:
1. **Reviewing the Dashboard:**
   - David logs into the interactive Plotly Dash UI (`main.py run-dashboard` -> `http://localhost:8050`).
   - **Showcase:** Show the live dashboard. Point out the KPI metrics, the geographical risk heatmaps, and the list of at-risk accounts.

2. **Investigating a High-Risk Flag:**
   - David sees "Sarah" flagged in his queue.
   - **Showcase:** Click on Sarah's profile. Tell the judges to look at the **SHAP Waterfall chart**. 
   - David doesn't have to guess why Sarah is risky; the system tells him exactly what variables contributed to the score.

3. **The Guided Call Script:**
   - The system automatically assigns a task to David's queue in the `rm_tasks` PostgreSQL table.
   - Attached to the task is a GenAI-generated call script.
   - **Showcase:** Show the script: *"Context: Customer had a recent medical event. Goal: Offer a 3-month EMI conversion for the hospital bill. Opening line: 'Hi Sarah, calling from Barclays to check in...'"*
   - *Hackathon Talking Point:* "The AI empowers the RM. It removes the friction of investigation and gives them the tools to resolve the issue on the first call, saving the bank's relationship with the customer."

---

## 👩‍🔬 Persona 4: Elena (The Data Scientist / Auditor)
**Goal:** Ensure the model is accurate, fair, explainable, and production-ready.

### Flow:
1. **Training & Hyperparameter Tuning:**
   - Elena runs `main.py train`.
   - **Showcase:** Show the pipeline logs where Optuna does hyperparameter tuning on the XGBoost and LightGBM models. Explain the ensemble approach (LSTM for temporal patterns, Trees for tabular features).

2. **Model Tracking & Registry:**
   - Elena opens the MLflow UI (`http://localhost:5000`).
   - **Showcase:** Show the MLflow dashboard. Point out the tracked metrics (AUC-ROC, F1-Score), the saved model artifacts, and the hyperparameter logs.

3. **Bias & Fairness Auditing (Crucial for Banking):**
   - Elena runs `main.py audit-fairness`.
   - **Showcase:** Show the fairness report generated in the console. The system checks if the model exhibits disparate impact based on age or location.
   - *Hackathon Talking Point:* "In financial services, high accuracy isn't enough; the model must be unbiased. We baked continuous fairness auditing into the CI/CD pipeline using AIF360 and Fairlearn to ensure regulatory compliance."

---

## 🚀 How to Execute This Demo Live

1. **Start the environment:** `python main.py up`
2. **Generate data:** `python main.py generate-customers 50`
3. **Show MLflow (The Brains):** Open `http://localhost:5000`
4. **Run the n8n Workflow (The Arteries):** Open `http://localhost:5678`, import `pdi_demo_workflow.json`, and hit "Execute Workflow" to show transactions streaming through the scoring API and triggering GenAI interventions.
5. **Show the Dashboard (The Face):** Open `http://localhost:8050` to see the results of the streaming data in real-time.
